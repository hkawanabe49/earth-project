How Biodiversity Affects Climate Change

Biodiversity can serve as a natural carbon sink, storing carbon dioxide so it doesn't affect our climates. Peatlands, mangroves, and seagrasses are all great at taking greenhouse gases for a healthy planet, and sea life can intake 4 times as much carbon as land life. Though more diverse places are often more resilient when attacked by unnatural disasters, places known as tipping points can exponentially add to the carbon dioxide in the atmosphere. This happens when places like the Amazon rainforest intake too much carbon dioxide, and instead start producing carbon dioxide instead.
