The Impacts of Climate Change on Biodiversity & How Biodiversity Can Help Prevent It

Biodiversity—the variety of life within ecosystems—plays a crucial role in regulating our planet’s climate. Rich, diverse habitats capture and store more carbon, stabilize weather patterns, and increase resilience to extreme events, helping to keep Earth’s temperature in check.

Climate Change: Rising Temperatures & Escalating Disasters

Climate change disrupts natural systems by driving up global temperatures and amplifying storms, droughts, and wildfires—threatening both human communities and wildlife across the globe.

A Two-Way Street: How They Influence Each Other

While robust biodiversity helps slow the advance of climate change, rising temperatures and extreme weather events driven by climate change, in turn, erode the very diversity we depend on—creating a dangerous feedback loop.
